Project 01

Beyond a Singular Dimension: A Multidimensional Trust Framework for Understanding User Intention and Usage of Large Language Models (Undergraduate Thesis)

Python R Data Analytics Statistical Modeling AI UI/UX

Overview

People keep using AI tools they don't fully trust. Our undergraduate thesis broke "trust" into six measurable dimensions to find which ones drive people to adopt an AI tool, and which keep them coming back.

  • Built a Python (pandas) pipeline that cleaned, merged, and validated 30 daily survey waves from 300+ participants into a single analysis-ready dataset
  • Modeled six trust dimensions with Covariance-Based SEM in R (lavaan): factor analysis, path modeling, mediation, and multi-group comparison
  • Validated UI design changes with a controlled A/B test, checked for significance with Welch's t-test
  • Built the study's data visualizations, from exploratory analysis to final results, in ggplot2 (R) and matplotlib (Python)
  • My role: data and statistics lead on a four-person team, owning the pipeline and modeling end to end

Framework & Method

Key Findings

Trust in "getting started" is not the same as trust in "staying."

  • All six dimensions drove initial adoption; only four (Emotional, Dispositional, History-Based, Institutional) sustained long-term use
  • Onboarding compounds: a user's general openness to technology fed long-term engagement by first strengthening intent
  • Emotional trust formed differently on mobile than on desktop, something product teams rarely account for
  • The redesigned UI beat the control group on five of six dimensions, plus both intent and engagement

Recommendations

Each dimension maps to a concrete design move:

  • Cognitive Trust: source citations, fact-check indicators, task-specific modes
  • Emotional Trust: personalization and tone controls, especially on mobile
  • Situational Trust: real-time confidence levels and clear, per-task limits
  • History-Based Trust: visible conversation history, summaries, and progress indicators
  • Institutional Trust: privacy and data practices surfaced in context, not buried in settings
  • Dispositional Trust: transparent, guided onboarding to convert skeptics early
